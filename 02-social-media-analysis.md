## Instagram Audit (@hakshan_)

### Current Performance

| Metric | Value | Benchmark (F&B, nano-tier Malaysia) |
|--------|-------|--------------------------------------|
| Followers | ~2,771 | 5,000–15,000 expected for a 5-branch chain |
| Posts | 348 | Good frequency baseline |
| Engagement Rate (est.) | ~2–4% | Typical for <10K accounts: 3–6% |
| Follower-to-Post Ratio | ~8 followers/post | Low — content not converting to follows |
| Bio | "让平凡的一餐，有着非凡的意义" | Strong — tagline-led, bilingual, emotional |

### Content Themes Observed

| Content Type | Frequency | Performance Notes |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Food photography (duck rice, pork) | High | Core content; visually strong dishes |
| Branch opening announcements | Moderate | High engagement during openings |
| Behind-the-scenes kitchen | Low | Highest potential — rarely used |
| Charity/CSR storytelling | Very Low | **Most powerful differentiator; underused** |
| Customer repost / UGC | Low | Missed opportunity |
| Festival & seasonal posts | Moderate | Good timing; needs stronger call-to-action |
| Team/staff stories | Very Low | Human interest angle untapped |

### Key Instagram Weaknesses

1. **Follower count severely lags behind branch count** — 5 physical outlets should translate to 8,000–15,000 followers organically
2. **Charity mission is rarely visible** — the most compelling story Hakshan has is barely told on Instagram
3. **No Reels strategy** — Instagram Reels drive 3x more reach than static posts; no consistent Reels presence detected
4. **Hashtag usage likely inconsistent** — Malay/Chinese/English community hashtags not maximised
5. **No Stories highlights** — key information like menus, branch addresses, and reviews not pinned
6. **No link in bio tool** — single website link limits ability to direct to all branches, menus, reservations

### What Is Working

- Brand tagline is strong and consistently communicated
- Food photography quality appears reasonable
- Multiple branch openings have generated community excitement
- Previous campaign achieved **400% follower and engagement increase** — proof the audience responds when properly activated

### Content Strategy Assessment

Facebook is performing relatively better than Instagram for Hakshan — likely because their target demographic (Chinese-Malaysian families, 30–55 age group) is more active on Facebook. However, the page still underperforms for a 5-branch chain with 3,000+ reviews.

**Facebook-specific opportunities:**
- Facebook Groups participation in local Kepong/Subang/Puchong food groups
- Running Facebook Events for festive dining (CNY, Ching Ming, etc.)

## TikTok — Critical Gap

### Current Situation
**No verified TikTok account found for Hakshan.**

This is the single biggest social media gap identified. TikTok is now the **#1 platform for F&B discovery in Malaysia among 18–35 year olds**.

### Why TikTok Matters for Hakshan

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Malaysian TikTok users** | 16+ million active users |
| **F&B content virality** | Food content is top-performing category |
| **Zero-cost reach** | New accounts can go viral without ad spend |
| **Hakka storytelling** | Heritage food content performs exceptionally well |
| **Duck rice visuals** | Braised sauces, steam, food porn — perfect for TikTok |
| **Charity angle** | Cause-driven content reliably generates shares |

### What TikTok Content Would Work

1. **"每一口的善" (Every bite of goodness)** — Show how each meal contributes to charity with a running counter
2. **Duck rice ASMR** — Slow-mo sauce pour, chopstick pull, steam rising — no words needed
3. **Chef stories** — "Why I make Hakka food" — authentic 60-second stories
4. **Before & after branch opening** — Document the full journey of a new branch
5. **Customer surprise moment** — Hidden camera customer reactions to learning their meal helps someone
6. **"Hakka Home Cooking vs Hakshan"** — compare grandma's home recipe with the restaurant's version

## Xiaohongshu (小红书) — Significant Gap

### Why It Matters

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Platform** | China's Instagram + Pinterest hybrid; used by Chinese-Malaysians & tourists |
| **F&B discovery** | Top platform for restaurant recommendations in Chinese communities |
| **Demographics** | Mandarin-speaking audience — Hakshan's core base |
| **Content type** | Photo essays, aesthetic food reviews, personal stories |
| **Viral mechanics** | Review posts by micro-KOLs get discovered for months/years |

### Recommended Approach
- Set up official brand account
- Seed the platform with 10–15 high-quality food posts
- Invite loyal customers to share their experience with a campaign hashtag
- Chinese New Year and charity-themed posts perform very well
