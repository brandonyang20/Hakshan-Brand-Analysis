## Brand Story

Hakshan was born from a belief that a meal can be more than just food — it can carry meaning, heritage, and kindness.

The name **客善 (Hak Shan)** draws from the Hakka word *客 (Hak / 客家 Hakka)* — the Chinese ethnic group known for their migratory history and resilient culture — and **善 (Shan)**, meaning *goodness, kindness, and virtue*. Together, the name embodies the spirit of **"Hakka goodness."**

What sets Hakshan apart from every other Chinese restaurant in Malaysia is its founding principle: **every meal served directly funds a charitable cause**. This makes Hakshan Malaysia's **first restaurant to structurally embed charitable giving into its dining model** — not as a one-off campaign, but as a core operating value.

The concept resonates deeply in the Hakka community, a people historically defined by resilience, community support, and the importance of sharing what you have with others.

### Menu Structure
- **Lunch sets:** Rice + 3 dishes + soup — value-focused, fast turnover
- **À la carte:** Hakka classics for group dining
- **Membership perk:** RM5 discount on à la carte items for members
- **Pricing:** Affordable — positioned as everyday dining, not a special occasion restaurant

### Dietary Profile
- **Non-halal** (pork-based dishes are core menu items)
- Targeting primarily Chinese-Malaysian demographic
- Limited vegetarian/vegan options noted (gap)

## Business Model Strengths

1. **Charity-driven USP** — Unique in the Malaysian F&B space; builds emotional loyalty
2. **Scalable format** — Standardised menu enables quality control across 5 branches
3. **Volume over premium** — Fast turnover, affordable pricing drives revenue through frequency
4. **Community embeddedness** — Located in residential Chinese neighbourhoods with repeat customer potential
5. **Positive word-of-mouth** — 3,000+ organic Google reviews without heavy paid advertising
