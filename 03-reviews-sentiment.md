## Sentiment Analysis — What People Are Saying

### Positive Sentiment Drivers (What People Love)

#### 1. Food Authenticity (Most Common Positive Theme)
- Repeatedly described as "authentic," "like home cooking," "real Hakka taste"
- Ginger-sprout braised duck is the signature hero — consistently described as tender, aromatic, well-balanced
- Older diners specifically note the nostalgic connection to traditional Hakka flavours
- Dishes described as using fresh, quality ingredients

#### 2. Value for Money (Second Most Common)
- Lunch sets are regularly praised as affordable yet generous
- Portions described as satisfying
- No complaints about overpricing — strong value perception across all branches
- Membership discount (RM5 off à la carte) adds perceived value

#### 3. Service Quality
- Frontline staff described as friendly and attentive
- Fast service even during busy periods — a significant operational strength
- Family-welcoming atmosphere noted across multiple branches

#### 4. Ambiance & Cleanliness
- "Comfortable, clean and unpretentious" — recurring phrase
- Not trying to be a fine-dining venue; positioned correctly for its target market
- Suitable for families, groups, casual dates, office lunches

#### 5. Charitable Mission (Emerging Positive Theme)
- When customers are aware of the CSR element, it creates a strong emotional positive reaction
- "Socially conscious diners who want meals that give back" identified as a key segment
- Awareness of this USP is still low — represents a significant marketing opportunity

---

### Negative Sentiment Drivers (What People Complain About)

#### 1. Wait Times During Peak Hours
- Weekend queues noted at the Kepong flagship
- More a sign of popularity than poor management, but can deter new customers
- No visible queue management system or pre-booking promotion

#### 2. Parking Challenges
- Common complaint at Kepong and USJ Taipan locations
- Shared with most suburban commercial area restaurants in Malaysia
- Limited ability to resolve without landlord coordination

#### 3. Limited Vegetarian/Vegan Options
- Pork-heavy menu is a deliberate choice but alienates potential diners
- A few vegetable side dishes available but not highlighted
- Growing vegetarian demographic in Malaysia partially excluded

#### 4. Inconsistency Between Branches (Minor)
- A few comments suggest slight variation in flavour/portions between branches
- Common challenge for any multi-outlet F&B chain
- Signals need for stronger standardisation SOPs

#### 5. Charity Awareness Gap (Indirect Negative)
- Not a complaint per se, but many diners are unaware of the charitable giving model
- Missed opportunity — if they knew, it would drive positive sentiment
- Found in review tone: generic positive reviews without referencing the USP

## Net Promoter Indicators

Based on public review data, Hakshan's implied Net Promoter Score (NPS) is estimated to be **high positive (60–75 range)** — indicating a strong proportion of "promoters" (customers who actively recommend) versus "detractors."

This is a significant asset that is not being systematically leveraged (e.g., no referral program, no "bring a friend" campaign, no UGC activation).

---

## Review Volume vs Social Following — The Gap

This is the most revealing data point in the entire analysis:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Google reviews (total) | 3,000+ |
| Instagram followers | ~2,771 |
| Facebook likes | ~6,257 |

**Hakshan has more Google reviews than Instagram followers.** This means customers are visiting and loving the food — but they're not being converted to social followers. The dining experience ends at the restaurant; there's no digital continuation of the relationship.

Every table is a potential social media audience member who never gets asked to follow.
