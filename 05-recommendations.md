# 05 — Recommendations & Marketing Strategy

## Strategic Framework

Hakshan's growth strategy should operate on three horizons:

| Horizon | Timeframe | Focus |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| **H1 — Quick Wins** | 0–30 days | Fix existing gaps; activate what's already there |
| **H2 — Growth Engine** | 1–3 months | Build new channels and campaigns |
| **H3 — Brand Building** | 3–12 months | Own the Hakka heritage brand space in Malaysia |

---

## H1 — Quick Wins (0–30 Days)

### 1. Fix Your Digital Foundations

#### Instagram
- [ ] Add **Linktree** or similar in bio → link to all 5 branches (Google Maps), menu, reservation (UMAI), Facebook
- [ ] Create **Story Highlights**: Menu | Our Branches | Charity | Reviews | Behind the Kitchen
- [ ] Update bio to include English translation of tagline for wider reach
- [ ] Start **replying to every comment** — even a simple "Thank you!" signals an active brand

#### Facebook
- [ ] Ensure all 5 branch pages are claimed, verified, and up-to-date with opening hours, address, phone
- [ ] Enable **Facebook Reviews** on all branch pages
- [ ] Add **Book Now** / **Order Food** CTAs to page header
- [ ] Respond to all existing unanswered comments/reviews

#### Google Business Profile
- [ ] Ensure all 5 branch profiles are **owner-claimed** and verified
- [ ] Upload 20+ high-quality photos per branch
- [ ] Add complete menu with prices
- [ ] Start responding to **every Google review** — positive and negative
  - Positive: personalise the thank-you, mention a dish they referenced
  - Negative: apologise, offer resolution, take it offline ("please contact us at...")
- [ ] Enable Google Q&A and answer existing questions

### 2. The Charity Story — Tell It Loudly

This is Hakshan's **single most powerful differentiator** and it is almost entirely invisible online. Fix this immediately.

**Actions:**
- [ ] Create a **"每一口的善" (Every Bite of Goodness)** recurring post format — monthly update showing total donations given, charity partner updates, community impact
- [ ] Add a **charity meter** or visual counter to the restaurant interior — "This month, your meals have helped X families" — photograph it for social
- [ ] Feature the **charity beneficiary** (with permission) in at least one Instagram/Facebook post per quarter — humanise the giving
- [ ] In restaurant: update table cards, receipts, or menu footer with "Your meal today contributed to [cause]"
- [ ] Train staff to mention the charity element naturally when taking orders — word-of-mouth starts here

---

### 5. Influencer Marketing Strategy

**Target profile:** Micro-KOLs (5K–50K followers) with food/lifestyle focus in KL/Selangor

**Why micro over mega:**
- 3–8x higher engagement rate vs macro influencers
- More authentic; their followers actually trust their food opinions
- More affordable: complimentary meal + small fee vs RM5,000–50,000 for a big name
- Better ROI measurability (trackable via unique promo codes)

**Recommended approach:**

| Tier | Follower Range | Budget | Expected | How Many |
|------|---------------|--------|----------|----------|
| Nano (gifted) | 1K–10K | Complimentary meal | 500–2,000 impressions each | 10–15/month |
| Micro (paid+gifted) | 10K–50K | RM200–500 + meal | 5,000–20,000 impressions each | 2–4/month |
| Mid-tier (paid) | 50K–200K | RM1,000–3,000 | 20,000–80,000 impressions | 1/quarter |

**Content brief for influencers:**
1. Must mention the charity element (not optional)
2. Feature at least one signature dish (duck rice preferred)
3. Use campaign hashtag (see below)
4. Post to both Instagram feed/Reels AND TikTok (cross-platform seeding)

**Target influencer categories:**
- KL food bloggers / food Instagrammers
- Chinese-Malaysian lifestyle content creators
- Mummy bloggers (family dining angle)
- University students in Subang/Kepong area (organic buzz)
- Charity and CSR-focused creators (unique angle)

### 6. Launch Campaign Hashtag

**Suggested campaign hashtag:**
- **#客善家人** (Hakshan Family) — for customer UGC
- **#每一口的善** (Every Bite of Goodness) — for charity-themed content
- **#HakshanMeals** — English-friendly, broad

**Campaign mechanic:** Every customer who posts a photo with #客善家人 and tags @hakshan_ gets entered into a monthly draw for a free meal for 4. Cost: 1 free meal per branch per month = ~RM80–120 total monthly budget. Expected impact: 50–200 UGC posts per month.

### 7. Xiaohongshu (小红书) Strategy

**Why Xiaohongshu for Hakshan:**
- Core audience: Mandarin-speaking Chinese-Malaysians aged 18–40
- Platform favours heritage food stories — Hakka cuisine is extremely well-received
- Long-tail discovery: posts indexed for months/years after publishing
- Lower competition vs Instagram for Malaysian restaurants

**Launch plan:**
1. Create brand account: 客善 Hakshan
2. Seed with 15 high-quality "photo diary" style posts in the first month
3. Content themes: duck rice close-ups, brand story in Chinese, charity giving stories, branch ambiance
4. Partner with 2–3 Xiaohongshu creators (Malaysian Chinese community) for seeding
5. Use Chinese hashtags: #马来西亚美食 #客家美食 #吉隆坡餐厅 #客善

### 8. Loyalty Programme

**Current status:** Basic membership with RM5 à la carte discount.

**Recommended upgrade:**

| Programme Element | Detail |
|------------------|--------|
| **Digital stamp card** | 10 visits = 1 free meal (via WhatsApp or simple app) |
| **Birthday privilege** | Free duck rice set on birthday month |
| **Referral reward** | Bring a new friend = RM10 credit for both |
| **Charity multiplier** | Double charity contribution on member visits — "your loyalty does more good" |
| **Branch challenge** | Visit all 5 branches = "善心旅行者" badge + RM20 voucher |

**Implementation:** Start with a WhatsApp-based stamp card (lowest barrier). Migrate to app or loyalty platform (StoreHub, Eber) once critical mass reached.

## H3 — Brand Building (3–12 Months)

### 10. Signature Campaign: "一碗有意义的饭" (A Bowl With Meaning)

**Concept:** A year-long brand campaign centred on the idea that Hakshan's meals carry meaning beyond nutrition — they carry heritage, they carry kindness, they carry community.

**Execution across touchpoints:**

| Channel | Activation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Social media** | Monthly charity impact posts with real numbers |
| **In-restaurant** | Heritage wall — story of Hakka people and Hakshan's founding |
| **Packaging** | Each takeaway box has a QR code → charity impact page |

| **PR** | Pitch story to The Edge, Says.com, Malay Mail — "Malaysia's first charity restaurant" |
| **Events** | Annual charity dinner — premium ticketed event, proceeds go to partner charity |

### 14. Menu Development Recommendations

| Opportunity | Idea | Rationale |
|-------------|------|-----------|
| **Vegetarian/Vegan menu** | Hakka-inspired plant-based options (yong tau foo, abacus seeds, vegetable braised tofu) | Captures growing vegetarian market; broadens customer base |
| **Hakka heritage storytelling menu** | Menu cards that explain the history and origin of each dish | Deeper brand engagement; educational; shareable |
| **Kids menu** | Small portions of non-spicy Hakka classics | Family dining — attracts parents who are the decision-makers |
| **Catering packages** | Corporate lunch delivery, event catering | Revenue diversification; corporate CSR alignment (companies donate via catering) |
| **Hakshan merchandise** | Branded tote bags, chopstick sets, charity-linked products | Brand touchpoints beyond dining; charity merchandise is high-margin |

## Summary: The Big Picture

Hakshan has everything a great Malaysian F&B brand needs:
- **Authentic product** that genuinely delights customers
- **Emotional brand story** that is unique in the market
- **Charitable mission** that creates loyalty beyond the meal
- **5 locations** providing scale and community reach
- **3,000+ positive reviews** — proof the market loves them

The single gap is **digital activation**. The brand's offline strength is not yet visible online. Every recommendation in this report is designed to close that gap — to ensure that the next 3,000 customers who fall in love with Hakshan in the restaurant also become Hakshan followers, advocates, and storytellers online.

> **The goal: turn Hakshan's best diners into Hakshan's best marketers.**
